Australian medical practitioners invest significant time and money building their practice websites — but how many have ever had their website reviewed for AHPRA and TGA compliance?

Most practitioners are diligent about checking their social media posts before they go live. But their website — often the highest-traffic touchpoint with prospective patients — can sit unchecked for years, quietly accumulating compliance risks that AHPRA investigators are increasingly looking for.

Today, SafePost is addressing that gap directly with the launch of the **Website Compliance Audit**.

## What is the Website Compliance Audit?

The Website Compliance Audit is a new SafePost feature that allows practitioners and practices to check their existing website content — page by page — against AHPRA's advertising guidelines and TGA therapeutic goods advertising rules.

It works through a guided six-step workflow inside the SafePost dashboard. Subscribers enter the URLs of up to six pages from their practice website. SafePost's AI then fetches and analyses each page, identifying potential compliance issues and returning severity-rated findings — High, Medium, and Low — alongside specific, actionable recommendations for each issue identified.

The result is a comprehensive, downloadable audit report that gives practitioners a clear picture of where their website stands, and exactly what needs to be addressed.

## Why website compliance matters more than ever

AHPRA's advertising obligations apply to all forms of health service advertising — not just social media posts. Your practice website is advertising. Your service descriptions are advertising. Your before and after galleries, your testimonials section, your pricing page, your blog — all of it falls within the scope of Section 133 of the Health Practitioner Regulation National Law.

Common website compliance issues identified by the SafePost audit include:

- Before and after photographs displayed without prominent results-variation warnings
- Superlative claims such as "leading", "best", or "Australia's most experienced"
- Patient testimonials that imply guaranteed outcomes
- Promotional offers and discounts without clearly stated terms and conditions
- Risk minimisation language that trivialises surgical or procedural risks
- TGA therapeutic goods advertising without mandatory health warnings

Many of these issues are present on practice websites that have never been formally reviewed. The Website Compliance Audit gives practitioners a structured, affordable way to identify and address them before a complaint is made.
